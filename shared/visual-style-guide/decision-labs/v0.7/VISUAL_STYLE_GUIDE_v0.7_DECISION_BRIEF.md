## Existing TAA Source

The HHH repository contains a 96 × 96 pixel-art TAA insignia.

Its recognizable structure includes:

- a compact circular silhouette;
- central grain or wheat imagery;
- surrounding temporal or orbital geometry;
- amber and cyan accents;
- strong vertical organization.

The source sprite should remain canonical to the game.

The curriculum still needs:

- scalable print adaptation;
- one-color version;
- grayscale version;
- small-size version;
- minimum-size and clear-space rules;
- approved placement;
- relationship to the new SAA sister mark.

## SAA Candidate Directions

### A · Solar Sprout

Combines:

- central living sprout;
- sun disc;
- orbit;
- compact circular field.

Strengths:

- clearest expression of Solar Agricultural Authority;
- strong small-size silhouette;
- directly distinct from TAA grain imagery;
- remains agricultural without relying on a greenhouse structure.

### B · Orbital Dome

Combines:

- greenhouse dome;
- sprout;
- orbital arc;
- solar point.

Strengths:

- strongest controlled-environment meaning;
- clearly space-agricultural;
- more operational and architectural.

Risk:

- more internal detail at small sizes.

### C · Solar Array

Combines:

- solar-panel grid;
- sprout;
- orbital or horizon arc.

Strengths:

- strongest technology-and-cultivation relationship;
- visually distinctive.

Risk:

- may read as a renewable-energy organization before an agricultural authority.

## Lead-Design Recommendation

### SAA Concept

**A · Solar Sprout**

It provides the clearest sister relationship:

- TAA: agriculture preserved through time;
- SAA: living agriculture sustained through the solar system.

### Institutional Master Strategy

**Dual-master system**

Use:

- vector curriculum master for ordinary print, PDF, headers, covers, and scalable digital use;
- one-color and grayscale vector variants;
- simplified vector small-size variant;
- pixel source mark only in explicitly game-derived or pixel-art contexts;
- matched pixel SAA variant after the vector insignia is approved.

This preserves game continuity without forcing low-resolution assets into every curriculum use.

### Section-Icon Density

**Priority sections only**

Use Phosphor icons for major recurring navigation points:

- Mission Brief
- Science or History Focus
- Evidence
- Mechanism
- CER
- Recommendation
- Debrief or Exit Ticket

Use technical labels without icons for ordinary subsections such as:

- Vocabulary
- Prediction
- local notes;
- minor instructions;
- repeated evidence rows.

This avoids attaching an icon to every heading.

### Proposed Section-Icon Vocabulary

| Function | Proposed Phosphor concept |
|---|---|
| Mission Brief | Satellite |
| Science / History Focus | Leaf, atom, or time/history context as appropriate |
| Vocabulary | Book Open Text |
| Prediction | Lightbulb |
| Evidence | Magnifying Glass |
| Mechanism | Gear |
| CER | Note Pencil |
| Recommendation | Wrench |
| Debrief / Exit | Check Square |
| Map / Timeline | Map Trifold |

Exact icon assignments may be corrected when a Phosphor glyph is unclear at the required print size.

### Semantic Markers

**Icon + technical label**

Use a small Phosphor icon and short technical label inside the approved left-rule callout.

Initial semantic vocabulary:

- Science Focus
- History Focus
- Established Science
- Documented Evidence
- Plausible Extrapolation
- Inferred or Debated
- Fictional Context
- Caution
- Optional
- Teacher Note
- Technical Fallback
- Confirmed Finding

The marker identifies the meaning; the callout geometry remains the v0.5 left-rule system.

### Publication-Status Marks

**Outlined technical stamp**

Use:

- rectangular shape;
- 3 px technical radius;
- no rotation;
- JetBrains Mono;
- one-color outline;
- explicit wording.

Primary publication states:

- Draft
- Review
- Approved
- Deprecated
- Archived
- Superseded

Document-role labels:

- Student Edition
- Teacher Edition
- Answer Key
- Accessible Version

Document-role labels are not institutional marks and do not replace the Mission Title Block role line.

## Insignia Placement

### First Page

Place the institutional insignia in the reserved Mission Title Block mark position.

It remains subordinate to the activity title.

### Continuation Page

Use the approved simplified small-size mark.

### Cover

A larger insignia may appear, but must not become a watermark behind body text.

### Footer

Do not repeat the institutional insignia in every footer.

## Minimum Variants Required

Each approved institutional insignia must eventually include:

1. primary color vector;
2. one-color dark vector;
3. one-color reversed vector;
4. grayscale vector;
5. simplified small-size vector;
6. pixel source or matched pixel adaptation;
7. accessible text identification.
