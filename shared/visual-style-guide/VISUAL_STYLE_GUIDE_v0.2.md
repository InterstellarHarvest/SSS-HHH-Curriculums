# 1. Purpose

This guide establishes a unified visual language for the *Space Sprout Sleuth* and *Hunger, Harvest, & History* curriculum projects.

The system must:

- present both curricula as professionally produced educational materials;
- preserve a recognizable connection to their shared fictional universe;
- distinguish SAA documents from TAA documents without making them appear unrelated;
- remain fully usable in grayscale;
- prioritize scientific and historical explanation over decoration;
- support ordinary classroom printing, photocopying, projection, and digital distribution;
- allow multiple authors or designers to create consistent pages without relying on undocumented taste.

The games are the investigative environments.

The curricula are the scientific, historical, and instructional documentation surrounding those environments.

Curriculum materials must not look like game merchandise, fan material, or lightly reformatted screenshots.

---

# 2. Core Design Position

## 2.1 Visual Formula

The shared curriculum system combines:

- NASA mission documentation;
- scientific field notebooks;
- government technical manuals;
- modern laboratory workbooks;
- archival research packets;
- restrained, selective in-universe details.

The resulting documents should feel credible in a classroom before their fictional context is noticed.

## 2.2 Priority Order

When design goals compete, use this order:

1. Readability
2. Instructional clarity
3. Accessibility
4. Diagrammatic explanation
5. Information organization
6. Institutional identity
7. Atmosphere
8. Decoration

Decoration must never displace explanation.

## 2.3 Visual Test

Every non-text visual element must do at least one of the following:

- explain information;
- organize information;
- identify the document or institution;
- direct attention;
- establish a necessary historical or scientific context.

If it does none of these, remove it.

# 3. Locked Project Decisions

The following decisions are active and must be treated as fixed unless this guide records a later revision.

## 3.1 Shared System

- Hybrid professional and in-universe visual style
- Moderate immersion
- Grayscale-first production
- NASA-inspired mission-document aesthetic
- Diagrams prioritized over decoration
- Mixed layouts, with panels used only where useful
- Blank bordered response boxes
- Phosphor icon family for interface-style symbols
- Selective reuse of game assets
- Thin, technical borders rather than decorative frames
- Professional typography rather than game or novelty fonts

## 3.2 Institutional Marks

- HHH uses the **Temporal Agricultural Archive (TAA)** insignia.
- SSS requires an **SAA sister insignia** built from the same design family.
- The existing TAA game sprite is a visual source, not automatically the final print master.
- The SAA sister insignia remains to be finalized in a later identity revision.

# 4. Canonical Institutional Terminology

## 4.1 Solar Agricultural Authority

**Canonical full name:** Solar Agricultural Authority  
**Canonical abbreviation:** SAA  
**Associated curriculum:** *Space Sprout Sleuth*

The SSS repository consistently uses **Solar Agricultural Authority**. Earlier curriculum-guide wording that expanded SAA as “Space Agricultural Authority” or “Space Agriculture Authority” was incorrect.

### Required Usage

Use:

> Solar Agricultural Authority (SAA)

Do not use:

- Space Agricultural Authority
- Space Agriculture Authority
- Solar Agriculture Authority
- Space Agronomy Authority

### Institutional Character

For curriculum visual purposes, SAA is presented as an international and interplanetary scientific authority operating across the solar system. It investigates plant-growth failures, develops agricultural understanding, and supports successful cultivation beyond Earth.

SAA materials should suggest:

- scientific field operations;
- controlled investigations;
- orbital and planetary research;
- evidence-based diagnosis;
- public-service technical authority;
- practical, contemporary mission work.

SAA must not be visually framed as:

- a military command;
- a police agency;
- a secret intelligence organization;
- a commercial space corporation;
- a child-oriented “junior agent” club.

Its authority comes from scientific competence and public responsibility.

## 4.2 Temporal Agricultural Archive

**Canonical full name:** Temporal Agricultural Archive  
**Canonical abbreviation:** TAA  
**Associated curriculum:** *Hunger, Harvest, & History*

The TAA is a Concord institution founded jointly by humanity and the Zhel’ii. Its mandate is to study, document, preserve, and protect pivotal moments in the agricultural histories of member species.

### Institutional Character

TAA materials should suggest:

- archival rigor;
- historical evidence;
- preservation of records;
- provenance and source comparison;
- continuity across time;
- careful observation;
- institutional memory.

TAA must not be visually framed as:

- fantasy time magic;
- steampunk history;
- a museum gift shop;
- an antiquarian society;
- a secret occult archive.

Its authority comes from disciplined documentation and protection of the historical record.

## 4.3 Curriculum Titles

Use the following exact forms:

- *Space Sprout Sleuth*
- *Hunger, Harvest, & History*

Requirements:

- Preserve the ampersand in *Hunger, Harvest, & History*.
- Preserve the comma after “Harvest.”
- Do not abbreviate curriculum titles in student-facing display headings unless space requires it.
- SSS and HHH may be used in internal documentation, file names, production notes, and compact metadata.

# 5. Source-of-Truth Hierarchy

When names, lore, or visual conventions conflict, use the following hierarchy:

1. Shipped game content and current repository documentation
2. This visual style guide
3. Approved curriculum templates and component files
4. Production notes
5. Older drafts, demonstrations, and exploratory assets

A curriculum document must not silently rewrite game lore.

A deliberate lore change requires:

- an explicit project decision;
- updates to the relevant game or canonical design documentation;
- an entry in this guide’s version history;
- replacement of outdated templates and assets.

# 6. Brand Architecture

## 6.1 Shared Curriculum Family

SSS and HHH belong to one visual family but represent different institutions.

The shared curriculum system is not a third fictional organization and must not receive an invented seal, department name, or authority mark.

Shared family traits include:

- the same typographic foundation;
- the same page grid;
- the same component geometry;
- the same grayscale hierarchy;
- the same Phosphor icon rules;
- the same accessibility standards;
- the same restrained mission-document tone;
- the same diagram conventions.

## 6.2 Institutional Layer

The institutional layer distinguishes the two curricula.

| Attribute | SSS / SAA | HHH / TAA |
|---|---|---|
| Primary function | Scientific investigation of plant growth beyond Earth | Documentation and protection of agricultural history |
| Visual emphasis | Operations, systems, diagnosis, environments | Archives, evidence, provenance, chronology |
| Document character | Mission packet or technical investigation file | Archive packet or historical field record |
| Spatial motif | Orbit, station, habitat, controlled system | Thread, sequence, record, continuity |
| Botanical motif | Living sprout, leaf, root, cultivation system | Grain, seed, lineage, preserved record |
| Preferred diagram families | System diagrams, cause-and-effect chains, experimental comparisons | Timelines, source comparisons, maps, evidence chains |
| Institutional tone | Operational and empirical | Archival and evidentiary |
| Shared qualities | Scientific, calm, methodical, restrained, credible | Scientific, calm, methodical, restrained, credible |

## 6.3 Relationship Rule

The two visual identities must feel like sister institutions within one future scientific culture.

They must not:

- use unrelated typographic systems;
- use radically different border geometry;
- use different icon families;
- use competing levels of immersion;
- appear to come from different publishers.

They should differ through:

- insignia;
- accent treatment;
- terminology;
- diagram emphasis;
- selected motif;
- document metadata.

## 6.4 Co-Branded Materials

A page may display both institutional marks only when its content genuinely connects both curricula or institutions.

Co-branded pages must:

- use a neutral shared header structure;
- give the two marks equal visual weight;
- keep the curriculum title more prominent than either mark;
- avoid inventing a combined SAA–TAA seal;
- remain visually balanced in grayscale.

Routine SSS pages should use only SAA identity.

Routine HHH pages should use only TAA identity.

# 7. Institutional Visual Character

## 7.1 SAA Visual Character

SAA pages should feel like documents issued by a future-facing scientific operations authority.

Preferred visual cues:

- compact mission identifiers;
- location and environment metadata;
- clean system diagrams;
- specimen or habitat callouts;
- evidence tables;
- measured technical framing;
- orbital or solar geometry used sparingly;
- botanical forms shown as scientific subjects.

Appropriate SAA labels include:

- Mission Brief
- Investigation File
- Observation Record
- Systems Diagram
- Evidence Summary
- Environmental Readings
- Findings
- Technical Note
- Specimen Record

Avoid overusing fictional operational language. A worksheet does not need to call every section a mission.

## 7.2 TAA Visual Character

TAA pages should feel like documents issued by a future archival research institution.

Preferred visual cues:

- archive or record identifiers;
- dates and chronology;
- source labels;
- provenance markers;
- evidence comparisons;
- timeline structures;
- restrained thread or continuity motifs;
- historical imagery treated as evidence rather than decoration.

Appropriate TAA labels include:

- Archive Brief
- Field Record
- Evidence Register
- Source Note
- Provenance Record
- Historical Sequence
- Findings
- Record Comparison
- Archive Reference

Avoid making every worksheet look like an aged artifact. The TAA interface is futuristic and professional even when its subject is ancient history.

## 7.3 Shared Educational Voice

Plain educational labels remain acceptable and are often preferable:

- Read
- Observe
- Compare
- Record
- Explain
- Analyze
- Draw
- Calculate
- Evidence
- Conclusion

Institutional language should reinforce the document, not obscure its purpose.

# 8. Moderate Immersion Standard

## 8.1 Definition

Moderate immersion means the fictional institutions are visible, but educational function remains immediately clear.

A student should understand what to do without decoding role-play language.

## 8.2 Acceptable Treatment

Acceptable:

> SAA Observation Record  
> Record two differences between the plant samples.

Acceptable:

> TAA Evidence Comparison  
> Compare the two accounts and identify where they disagree.

Acceptable:

> Mission Reference: L2-HAB-04

## 8.3 Excessive Treatment

Avoid:

> Cadet, prove your worth to the Authority by unlocking the secret specimen file!

Avoid:

> Temporal Operative Clearance Required

Avoid:

> Your heroic mission begins now!

Avoid fake bureaucracy that adds no instructional value.

## 8.4 Insufficient Treatment

A document is also off-system when all institutional identity is removed and it resembles a generic worksheet assembled from unrelated classroom templates.

At minimum, a curriculum page should normally include:

- curriculum identification;
- appropriate SAA or TAA identity;
- consistent shared typography and geometry;
- a controlled header or footer system.

# 9. Insignia System

## 9.1 TAA Insignia: Existing Source

The existing TAA game insignia is a 96 × 96 pixel-art mark. Its composition combines:

- a central grain or wheat form;
- enclosing temporal or orbital arcs;
- a cool-cyan and amber dual-accent palette;
- a compact, near-circular silhouette;
- a technical, future-archive character.

The insignia successfully combines agriculture, chronology, and institutional authority.

## 9.2 TAA Curriculum Mark Status

The current game sprite may be used as a visual reference and may appear selectively in pixel-art contexts.

It must not automatically be treated as the universal curriculum master because:

- its native resolution is small;
- its pixel detail may not reproduce clearly at all print sizes;
- a grayscale version has not yet been formally optimized;
- clear-space and minimum-size rules have not yet been finalized;
- a vector or high-resolution print adaptation may be required.

A curriculum-ready TAA mark set should eventually include:

1. Primary color mark
2. One-color dark mark
3. One-color light/reversed mark
4. Grayscale mark
5. Simplified small-size mark
6. Pixel-art source mark for explicitly game-derived contexts

## 9.3 SAA Sister Insignia: Design Requirement

SSS requires a sister insignia that clearly belongs to the same institutional family as the TAA mark.

The SAA insignia must share:

- comparable visual density;
- a compact near-circular or vertically balanced silhouette;
- a central botanical symbol;
- enclosing scientific or spatial geometry;
- similar line-weight logic;
- similar small-size legibility;
- compatible monochrome behavior.

The SAA insignia must remain distinct by using:

- a living sprout, leaf, or cultivation motif rather than a grain-record motif;
- orbital, solar, habitat, or growth-system geometry rather than a temporal spiral;
- an operational scientific character rather than an archival character.

### Provisional Visual Territory

The preferred design territory is:

> A central emerging sprout or leaf enclosed by a simplified orbital path or solar-system arc.

This is a design brief, not final artwork.

The final mark must avoid:

- generic recycling-symbol geometry;
- military wings;
- shield-heavy law-enforcement imagery;
- rockets as the dominant symbol;
- overly literal planet-and-leaf clip art;
- resemblance to the NASA “meatball” mark;
- confusing similarity to the TAA wheat-and-thread composition.

## 9.4 Insignia Relationship

Viewed side by side, the marks should communicate:

- TAA: agriculture preserved through time
- SAA: agriculture sustained beyond Earth

They should appear designed by the same civilization and reproduced by the same curriculum publisher.

## 9.5 Provisional Placement Rules

Until full insignia specifications are completed:

- Place one institutional mark in the upper-left or upper-right document identity zone.
- Do not repeat the mark as a watermark on every page.
- Do not place the mark inside student response boxes.
- Do not stretch, skew, rotate, outline, recolor arbitrarily, or add effects.
- Keep the mark subordinate to the curriculum or document title.
- Use one-color versions for ordinary grayscale printing.
- Avoid placing detailed pixel versions below a size where their internal structure becomes unclear.

# 10. Shared Visual Language

## 10.1 Tone

Documents should feel:

- calm;
- clean;
- methodical;
- scientific;
- credible;
- durable;
- information-rich without being crowded.

Avoid:

- comic-book framing;
- cartoon worksheet decoration;
- fake parchment;
- torn-paper edges;
- grunge;
- faux typewriter distress;
- excessive interface chrome;
- neon science-fiction styling;
- game HUD imitation.

## 10.2 Borders

Borders should be:

- thin;
- straight;
- technical;
- consistent;
- used to organize rather than decorate.

Permitted directions include:

- single-line frames;
- subtle double rules;
- corner registration marks;
- controlled metadata bands;
- open-sided technical dividers.

Rounded “app card” styling should not become the default page language.

## 10.3 Panels

Use panels when they improve grouping or hierarchy.

Appropriate panel uses:

- document metadata;
- evidence summaries;
- vocabulary;
- teacher guidance;
- warnings;
- source notes;
- compact data displays.

Do not place every paragraph in a separate box.

A page composed entirely of competing panels loses hierarchy and becomes visually noisy.

## 10.4 Response Areas

Student response areas must be:

- blank;
- rectangular;
- clearly bordered;
- sized for the expected response;
- visually separate from explanatory text;
- printable without color.

Do not use:

- notebook lines by default;
- decorative parchment;
- handwriting fonts;
- speech bubbles;
- game-dialog boxes;
- patterned fills.

## 10.5 Diagrams

Diagrams take precedence over decorative imagery.

Preferred diagram forms include:

- labeled scientific illustrations;
- systems diagrams;
- cross sections;
- experimental comparisons;
- process flows;
- cause-and-effect chains;
- timelines;
- maps;
- evidence relationships.

A decorative game image must not displace a diagram that would teach the concept more effectively.

---

# 11. Icon Architecture

## 11.1 Phosphor Icons

Phosphor is the locked icon family for general interface-style symbols.

Use Phosphor for:

- navigation;
- common actions;
- status indicators;
- document controls;
- familiar instructional cues;
- simple metadata labels.

## 11.2 Separation of Systems

Phosphor icons are not substitutes for:

- SAA or TAA insignia;
- scientific diagrams;
- historical source imagery;
- project-specific botanical symbols;
- game rank badges.

A generic icon should not replace a scientifically meaningful illustration when the subject itself needs to be shown.

## 11.3 Current Limitation

Exact icon weight, size, alignment, and semantic assignments will be locked in a later revision.

Until then:

- use one consistent Phosphor family and weight within a document;
- do not mix outline and filled icons casually;
- pair unfamiliar icons with text labels;
- do not use icons as scattered decoration.

---

# 12. Game-Asset Relationship

## 12.1 Principle

Game assets may support curriculum identity, but curriculum pages must not resemble captured game screens.

## 12.2 Approved Roles

Game-derived assets may serve as:

- specimen illustrations;
- character or institutional reference images;
- evidence images;
- small contextual figures;
- visual links between curriculum and game;
- sources for redrawn scientific diagrams.

## 12.3 Disallowed Default Uses

Do not:

- reproduce whole game interfaces as page layouts;
- use dialogue boxes as ordinary text containers;
- fill pages with rank badges or game icons;
- use pixel-art scenes as decorative banners without instructional purpose;
- enlarge low-resolution assets with smoothing;
- place unrelated sprites around page margins.

## 12.4 Institutional Marks

The TAA insignia and future SAA insignia are identity assets, not decorative game assets. Their use must follow the insignia system rather than general asset-reuse rules.

# 13. Document Identification

## 13.1 Required Identity

A finished curriculum page or packet should normally identify:

- curriculum title;
- issuing institution where appropriate;
- document or activity title;
- page number when part of a packet.

## 13.2 Optional Metadata

Depending on document type, metadata may include:

- document code;
- mission or archive reference;
- location;
- date or historical period;
- version;
- student name and class;
- teacher-only designation.

Metadata must remain compact and subordinate to the learning material.

## 13.3 Sample SAA Header Logic

```text
[SAA MARK]  SPACE SPROUT SLEUTH
SOLAR AGRICULTURAL AUTHORITY · OBSERVATION RECORD

Plant Growth Under Altered Gravity
Reference: SSS-GRV-02
```

## 13.4 Sample TAA Header Logic

```text
[TAA MARK]  HUNGER, HARVEST, & HISTORY
TEMPORAL AGRICULTURAL ARCHIVE · EVIDENCE RECORD

Comparing Accounts of Agricultural Change
Archive Reference: HHH-EVD-03
```

These examples establish hierarchy only. Exact typography and measurements will be specified later.

# 14. Identity Quality-Control Checklist

A curriculum visual passes the v0.2 identity review when all answers are **yes**.

## 14.1 Canonical Naming

- [ ] SAA is expanded as **Solar Agricultural Authority**.
- [ ] TAA is expanded as **Temporal Agricultural Archive**.
- [ ] Curriculum titles use exact spelling and punctuation.
- [ ] No document silently introduces alternate organization names.

## 14.2 Shared Family

- [ ] The document looks related to the other curriculum.
- [ ] Institutional differences are visible without changing the entire design system.
- [ ] The page remains professional before its fictional context is considered.
- [ ] The design does not imitate a game screen.

## 14.3 Institutional Fit

- [ ] SAA materials emphasize scientific operations, systems, and investigation.
- [ ] TAA materials emphasize evidence, records, provenance, and chronology.
- [ ] Institutional language supports rather than obscures the task.
- [ ] The insignia is used as identification rather than decoration.

## 14.4 Immersion

- [ ] The activity remains immediately understandable.
- [ ] No role-play language replaces clear instructions.
- [ ] The page contains enough institutional identity to belong to the project.
- [ ] Decorative science-fiction elements do not interfere with reading or printing.
