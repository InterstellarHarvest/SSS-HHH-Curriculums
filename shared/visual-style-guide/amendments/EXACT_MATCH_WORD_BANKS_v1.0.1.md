# Exact-Match Word Banks — Approved Clarification

**Decision:** Owner approved  
**Applies to:** SSS and HHH Student and Accessible worksheets  
**Status:** Required production rule

## Locked rule

Use a word bank for a constrained fill-in-the-blank activity when students could understand the concept but still struggle to generate the exact expected word or phrase from memory.

The bank must contain exactly the answer entries used in that activity:

- one entry for every blank;
- no decoy entries;
- no extra vocabulary;
- no omitted answer;
- repeated entries when the same answer is required more than once;
- intact phrases when a blank requires a phrase rather than one word.

The bank may be ordered differently from the blanks, but it must not add difficulty through unrelated distractors.

## Placement and appearance

- Place the bank immediately before or after the blanks it supports, on the same page and within the same task section.
- Label it **WORD BANK**.
- Use a compact ruled or pale-neutral technical treatment that survives grayscale and photocopying.
- Do not use drag-and-drop as the only access method.
- Standard and Accessible editions use the same answer set; the Accessible bank may wrap or enlarge for readability.
- The Answer Key must use the same terms or phrases exactly.

## Case 01 approved bank

For **Task 5 · Build the mechanism**, the exact four entries are:

1. `curve or grow without consistent orientation`
2. `downward`
3. `settle`
4. `settle in one direction`

Each entry is used once. There are four blanks and four bank entries.

## Required regression checks

After adding a word bank to an existing packet, validate:

- exact one-to-one correspondence between bank entries and blanks;
- matching Standard, Accessible, and Answer Key wording;
- reading order and accessible labeling;
- Student and Accessible page counts;
- overflow in every role;
- grayscale and print-preview output;
- no changes to existing response persistence keys.
