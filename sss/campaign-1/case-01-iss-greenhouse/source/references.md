# References - ISS Greenhouse Module

## Scientific and technical sources

1. NASA. **Growing Plants in Space.** Describes Veggie plant pillows, fluid distribution challenges in microgravity, and the use of environmental cues such as light.  
   https://www.nasa.gov/exploration-research-and-technology/growing-plants-in-space/

2. NASA Ames Research Center. **Plant Gravity Perception (SpaceX-13).** Describes statolith-based gravity perception, reduced gravity responses, and ISS plant research.  
   https://www.nasa.gov/ames/space-biosciences/plant-gravity-perception-spacex-13/

3. NASA. **Veggie Plant Growth System Activated on International Space Station.** Documents Veggie activation, plant pillows, LEDs, growth media, and the Veg-01 lettuce crop.  
   https://www.nasa.gov/missions/station/veggie-plant-growth-system-activated-on-international-space-station/

4. NASA Science. **Plant Biology Program - Hardware.** Overview of Veggie and Advanced Plant Habitat systems.  
   https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/focus-areas/plant-biology/hardware/

5. Nakamura, M. et al. **Settling for Less: Do Statoliths Modulate Gravity Perception?** Plants, 2020.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31963631/

6. NASA Open Science Data Repository. **Proteomics and Transcriptomics analysis of Arabidopsis Seedlings in Microgravity (OSD-38).**  
   https://osdr.nasa.gov/bio/repo/data/studies/OSD-38
